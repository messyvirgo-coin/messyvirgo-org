# **ARTICLES OF ASSOCIATION**

**Association “Messy Virgo DAO”, Version 1**

## **I. NAME, SEAT, AND PURPOSE**

### **Art. 1: Name and Seat**

1. An association exists under the name **"Messy Virgo DAO"** in accordance with Art. 60 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code (ZGB).  
2. The registered office of the Association is located in **Zurich, Switzerland**.  
3. The Association is established for an indefinite duration.

### **Art. 2: Purpose**

1. The Association is an organization that promotes the convergence of institutional Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Decentralized Finance (DeFi). The core mission is to democratize access to advanced financial intelligence according to the guiding principle "Complexity, Simplified."  
2. The purpose is realized in particular through the following activities:  
   * **Research and Development:** Supporting the creation of open-source frameworks for autonomous agents, including market data hubs, analysis engines, and tools for algorithmic portfolio optimization.  
   * **Human-in-the-Loop Governance:** Promoting a governance model where community sovereignty guides automated systems, ensuring that AI agents operate within human-defined ethical and risk-related boundaries.  
   * **Educational Initiatives:** Simplifying complex technical and financial concepts to make next-generation DeFi accessible to all users.  
   * **Ecosystem Stewardship:** Guiding the MESSY ecosystem toward full decentralization, including the management of community treasuries and the progressive handover of control to a DAO structure.  
   * **Economic Activity:** To ensure the long-term fulfillment of the Association's purpose, the Association is authorized to provide commercial services (e.g., operation of SaaS products, paid analysis or infrastructure services, fees for funds management). The Association may provide these services itself or have them performed by legally independent partners or subsidiaries.  
3. The Association is not primarily profit-oriented. Any generated surpluses are primarily used to strengthen the Association, to compensate for services rendered, and to expand the ecosystem.

## **II. RESOURCES**

### **Art. 3: Resources**

1. The Association's resources consist of:  
   * a) Revenue from economic activities (SaaS, subscriptions, fees).  
   * b) Donations, grants, and sponsoring contributions.  
   * c) Income from token holdings.  
   * d) Any membership fees.  
2. The Association may hold and manage digital assets.

## **III. MEMBERSHIP**

### **Art. 4: Admission and Termination**

1. **Admission:** Natural and legal persons may become members. The Board decides on admission requests at its own discretion.  
2. **Resignation:** Resignation is possible at any time. There is no claim to the Association's assets.  
3. **Exclusion:** A member may be excluded by the Board or the General Assembly if there are important reasons.

### **Art. 5: Liability**

Only the Association's assets are liable for the Association's debts. Any personal liability of the members is excluded.

## **IV. ORGANIZATION**

### **Art. 6: Organs**

The organs of the Association are:

* A. The General Assembly (GA)  
* B. The Board  
* C. The Auditors (if elected)

### **Art. 7: General Assembly (GA)**

1. The GA is the supreme organ. It takes place annually.  
2. It can be held physically or purely virtually. Resolutions can also be passed electronically (circular resolution).  
3. Each member has one vote. Resolutions are passed by a simple majority of the votes cast.

### **Art. 8: Board**

1. The Board consists of at least 2 persons. Term of office: 2 years (unlimited re-election possible).  
2. It manages the business and represents the Association externally.  
3. **Compensation:** Board members are entitled to appropriate compensation for their operational activities, provided the economic situation of the Association permits this. The General Assembly decides on the principle and framework of this compensation; the specific details may be laid down in a regulation approved by the General Assembly.  
4. **Signing Authority:** The Board regulates the signing authority (joint signature of two for amounts from CHF 25,000).

## **V. FINANCES AND AUDITING**

### **Art. 9: Accounting**

The Board is responsible for proper accounting in accordance with Swiss principles (Code of Obligations, OR).

### **Art. 10: Auditors**

1. The Association waives the appointment of an auditor (opting-out) as long as there is no legal obligation to do so.  
2. However, the General Assembly may at any time decide to elect one or two auditing bodies (members or external) to audit the accounts.

## **VI. SPECIFIC PROVISIONS**

### **Art. 11: DAO Governance and Tokens**

1. The Association manages token holdings in a fiduciary capacity in the interest of the community. Tokens held by the Association shall not be used to exercise voting rights in token-based on-chain governance (DAO), unless a regulation approved by the General Assembly provides otherwise for clearly defined cases.  
2. The General Assembly may adopt a regulation providing that certain decisions are pre-discussed by a token-based on-chain governance (DAO) or—to the extent compatible with mandatory law and these Articles of Association—act as binding instructions to the Association's organs.

### **Art. 12: Liquidation**

In the event of dissolution, the GA decides on the use of the assets in accordance with the Association's purpose.

## **VII. FINAL PROVISIONS**

### **Art. 13: Jurisdiction**

The place of jurisdiction is Zurich. Swiss law applies.
